Job-Market Outlook

How this field is evolving across institutions

Over the course of our research and interview with Edwin Rodarte at LAPL, we learned that oftentimes, specialized positions do not currently exist at many institutions, especially in the cases of emerging technology services and A.I. research, adoption, and education. Edwin recommends LIS students and emerging professionals to advocate for the creation of these specialized positions, and so do we! In searching for “emerging technologies” related positions, we found that certain skillsets, daily tasks, and required training were shared among a variety of different titled positions around the country. This range of job postings further proves that the field of emerging technologies in library work is versatile, vital, and growing.

Below are just a few of these job postings, both current and past, so LIS students and professionals interested in this field of work can get a better idea of the tasks and training required of professionals in these roles.

Shared Task Overview

  • A.I. research, assessment, implementation, and education
  • Community engagement and outreach involving technology equipment, services, and education
  • Supports tech operations / provides tech support and public assistance
  • Researches and supports new policies for emerging technologies
  • Administrative duties
  • Public engagement / events coordination

Shared Skill Overview

  • Knowledge of emerging trends and research in libraries and higher education
  • Interpersonal communication skills
  • Ability to master new tech quickly
  • Ability to work with diverse groups
  • Commitment to professional improvement

Shared Training Requirement Overview

  • MLIS from an ALA-accredited institution (or an equivalent professional degree)
