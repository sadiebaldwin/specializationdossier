Professional Context

Associations, forums, job boards, and research

Edwin Rodarte at LAPL is part of a nascent but growing profession. The challenge related to emerging technologies librarianship is mainly that the professional context shifts rapidly and continuously. Associations, conferences, journals, and forums that fall one or two years behind may as well be out-of-date by ten or twenty. It appears that a leading textbook, according to Science Direct, was published in 2016; it makes no explicit mention of AI. Most helpful, by far, is to know or make contact with a person already in the field. That being said, there are some resources that are highly useful to consult, at least to start:

Starting Points

  • ALA — Reference & User Services Association (RUSA), Emerging Technology Section
  • RUSA Update — Emerging Technology Section news
  • Code4Lib Jobs — Emerging Technologies tag
  • EIFL Training Programme — Artificial Intelligence & Open Science (for librarians)
  • IFLA Journal (Vol. 51, Iss. 5, Sept. 2025) — “Artificial intelligence in libraries: the emerging research agenda”
