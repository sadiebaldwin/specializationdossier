Los Angeles Public Library

Emerging Technologies in Public Libraries

Sadie Baldwin, Benjamin Ehrlich, Melayah Fawaz, Caitlin Fisher
Specialization Dossier
INF STD 270

This dossier synthesizes information gathered from an interview with LA Public Library’s Emerging Technologies Librarian Edwin Rodarte, as well as other external research. Through engaging with this site, we hope that users like LIS students and other professionals in the fields of Library and Information Services better understand the field and professional context and scope of “emerging technologies” as they apply to libraries.

Our largest takeaway from this project has been a greater understanding of the essentiality of community access to technology that libraries facilitate. As users will hopefully come to appreciate as they engage with this dossier, the scope of library technologies is vast, constantly growing, and always changing. It is the job of libraries and library professionals to both understand the tools and services at their disposal and engage with and educate their patrons on how to utilize them.

What We Learned

  • “Emerging technologies” is a broad field that covers many different skills, systems, and technologies.
  • Most skills in this field are learned on the job as opposed to in an MLIS program.
  • Libraries play a crucial role in educating on AI — now and in the future.
  • Public education and outreach for tech and tech services at the library is vital.
  • E-lending is the future of libraries.
  • Budget constraints shape the scope and abilities of work.

What Are Emerging Technologies?

  • Not always shiny and new tools — this field most often involves adapting tried and tested technology for accessible public use.
  • Makerspace tech, digital literacy, lending technology, and related services.
  • Infrastructure-oriented technology like after-hours lockers and behind-the-scenes data analytics.
